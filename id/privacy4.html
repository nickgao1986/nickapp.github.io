Network Rescue

Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 19, 2026

1. Overview

Network Rescue (the App) is a macOS utility that helps you measure and understand your internet connection. It can run speed tests, inspect local network details, check Wi-Fi signal information, look up DNS records, trace routes, scan selected ports, check website latency, generate connection reports, and offer guided troubleshooting suggestions.

This Privacy Policy explains what information the App processes on your Mac, how that information is used, and the choices you have. By using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

2. Who We Are

Network Rescue is developed and published as an independent Mac App Store application. For privacy questions or support requests, contact us at youjiancau@gmail.com.

3. Information We Process

The App does not create user accounts, does not require sign-in, and does not upload your speed-test history or local files to developer-operated servers for advertising, profiling, or identity tracking.

Depending on the features you use, the App may process the following information on your device or through networks you choose to contact:

  • Speed-test results and history: download and upload rates, latency, jitter, packet loss, timestamps, selected test servers, and related summary values. These remain on your Mac for display, comparison, and optional export or sharing that you initiate.
  • Network and connection details: local IP address, public IP address when requested, gateway, DNS servers, interface status, and related path information used to show Network Details and diagnostic reports.
  • Wi-Fi identity and signal context: when macOS grants Location Services permission, the App may read Wi-Fi name (SSID), BSSID, channel, and band so it can display Wi-Fi details. The App does not use this permission to track your geographic location for advertising or analytics.
  • Diagnostics you trigger: DNS lookups, traceroute hops, port checks, CDN lookups, website latency checks, multi-server comparisons, scheduled in-session tests, and Connection Rescue findings.
  • App preferences: settings such as selected servers, internet-plan reference values, free-test usage counters, and local purchase unlock state.
  • Purchase state: when you buy or restore the one-time Pro upgrade, Apple processes the transaction through StoreKit and the App Store. The App stores a local purchase flag so Pro features can remain unlocked on that Mac.

4. How We Use Information

Information processed by the App is used only to:

  • Provide speed testing, diagnosis, monitoring, reporting, and export features you request
  • Display results, history, and recommendations inside the App
  • Remember your preferences and Pro unlock status on this Mac
  • Communicate with the remote endpoints required for a feature you start, such as a test server or DNS-related lookup

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use the App to build advertising profiles.

5. Where Data Is Stored

Speed-test history, preferences, and local unlock state are stored on your Mac using local app storage such as UserDefaults and related on-device files. The App does not automatically sync that history to a developer-operated cloud account.

If you export a report, image, or text file, the file is saved only to the location you choose through the standard macOS save dialog.

6. Network Access

Core features require network access. When you run a speed test or diagnostic action, the App contacts the endpoints needed to complete that action. Examples include:

  • Speed-test download and upload endpoints you select in Settings
  • Public sites or hosts you choose for latency, DNS, traceroute, port, or CDN checks
  • Third-party IP lookup services used by CDN Lookup, such as ip-api.com, which may receive the target IP being queried
  • Public IP lookup services used when Network Details requests an external IP
  • Apple App Store / StoreKit services when you purchase or restore Pro

Scheduled tests run only while the App is open. Closing the App stops scheduled testing.

7. Permissions

  • Network: required for speed tests and diagnostic network requests.
  • Location Services: requested only because modern macOS requires Location authorization before an app can read Wi-Fi name and related Wi-Fi identity details. The App uses this to show Wi-Fi information. It does not collect or share your physical location for marketing.
  • Notifications: optional, and used only if you enable scheduled-test alerts while the App is running.
  • File access: limited to files and folders you explicitly choose when exporting or saving results.

You can change Location and Notification permissions in System Settings at any time. If Location access is denied, Wi-Fi name and related fields may remain unavailable.

8. Third-Party Services

The current version does not include ads, analytics SDKs, or social login SDKs.

Some features rely on infrastructure outside the App:

  • Apple: App Store distribution, StoreKit purchases, and related Apple account purchase restoration
  • Speed-test and probe endpoints: contacted only when you start a test or diagnostic action
  • ip-api.com: used by CDN Lookup for geolocation-style metadata about a queried IP address

Those services process only the data needed to complete the request you initiated. Their own privacy practices may also apply.

12. Your Choices and Deletion

13. Data Retention

Local history and preferences remain on your Mac until you delete them, clear them in the App, or remove the App. Exported files remain wherever you saved them. Apple retains purchase records according to Apple policies.

14. Security

15. International Users

18. FAQ

Does Network Rescue upload my speed-test history to your servers?

No. History is kept on your Mac by default. During a test, the App must communicate with the selected test endpoint to measure performance, but the result history is local unless you export or share it yourself.

Why does Network Rescue ask for Location permission?

macOS requires Location Services authorization before an app can read Wi-Fi name and related Wi-Fi identity details. The App uses that permission to show Wi-Fi information, not to track your location for ads.

How do I delete local data?

Use History or Settings inside the App to remove saved results. You can also uninstall the App. Exported files must be deleted manually from the folder where you saved them.

What if a test fails or a result looks wrong?

Email support and include your macOS version, app version, screenshots if available, and steps to reproduce the issue.

Summary: Network Rescue is a local-first macOS network testing and diagnosis app. It does not require an account, does not use ads or profiling SDKs, and stores test history on your Mac. Network requests happen when you run features that need them. Location permission is used only to access Wi-Fi identity details required by macOS.
